# Uniswap Ambassador Interview Preparation Guide
## (Business/Marketing Focus)

## Context
- **Meeting Type**: First-time introduction
- **Interviewer Background**: Business/Marketing (not technical)
- **Your Status**: Solo developer, no industry job/reputation
- **Project Stage**: Conceptual/design phase with extensive documentation
- **Primary Goals**: Market validation, user research guidance, community building strategy, ecosystem fit assessment

---

## Interview Structure

### Part 1: Introduction & Value Proposition (5-10 min)

**Your Opening** (Use business language, not tech jargon):

*"I'm building tooling that uses Uniswap v4 hooks to design custom tax mechanisms over pool economic agents. The core insight is that protocol engineers who are familiar with tax terminology will prefer to deploy pools here because we facilitate learning new crypto-specific terminology through more familiar economic terms."*

**The Specific Problem**: "The initial proof of concept focuses on solving a critical issue in Uniswap pools: revenue concentration. Currently, Just-In-Time (JIT) liquidity providers - who only provide liquidity for a single transaction - are capturing most of the trading fees, while Persistent Liquidity Providers (PLPs) - who commit long-term - get very little. This creates an unfair system where short-term extractors benefit more than long-term supporters."

**The Value Proposition**: "Instead of forcing protocol engineers to learn complex crypto terminology like 'fee deltas' and 'balance deltas', we provide familiar tax terminology like 'progressive taxation' and 'Pigouvian taxes' that maps to the underlying hook mechanics. This makes it easier for economists and protocol designers to implement sophisticated pool behaviors."

---

### Part 2: Market & Problem Validation (20-25 min)

#### Q1: Target Market Identification
**Question**: "From your work with the Uniswap community, what types of protocol engineers and economists are working on custom pool designs? Are you seeing teams with economic/finance backgrounds struggling to translate their ideas into hook implementations?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Identifies if there's a market for economic terminology translation
- Understands the background of current pool designers
- Validates the learning curve problem
- Gets real market data from her community experience

**Follow-up**: "Have you heard protocol engineers express frustration about having to learn crypto-specific terminology when they're already experts in economic theory?"

---

#### Q2: Pain Points & User Friction
**Question**: "When protocol engineers and economists try to implement custom pool behaviors, what are the main friction points? Is it the learning curve of crypto terminology, the complexity of hook mechanics, or something else? Specifically, have you heard concerns about the gap between economic theory and technical implementation?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Validates your core problem hypothesis about terminology barriers
- Identifies if the economic-to-technical translation is the real barrier
- Reveals other barriers protocol engineers face
- Gets qualitative validation from community interactions
- Specifically validates the learning curve problem

**Follow-up**: "Do you think protocol engineers would prefer to work with familiar economic terms rather than having to learn crypto-specific terminology?"

---

#### Q3: Ecosystem Positioning & Strategy
**Question**: "Uniswap v4 hooks enable lots of innovation. From a marketing and adoption perspective, what types of hook projects or use cases are most exciting for the ecosystem? What would make the biggest impact on v4 adoption?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Understands UF's strategic priorities
- Positions your work within ecosystem goals
- Identifies if 'facilitating economic expertise' aligns with UF vision
- Gets business-level strategic validation

**Your Framing**: "I see my tooling as an expertise accelerator - it helps protocol engineers with economic backgrounds contribute their knowledge to the v4 ecosystem more effectively, which could lead to more sophisticated and economically sound pool designs."

---

### Part 3: Community & User Research Strategy (20-25 min)

#### Q4: Access to Potential Users
**Question**: "As a solo developer without industry connections, I need to validate this with protocol engineers and economists who work on pool design. What channels or communities would you recommend for reaching these experts in the Uniswap ecosystem?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Gets practical user research strategy
- Addresses your credibility/access gap directly
- Opens potential for UF facilitation
- Shows humility and user-focused mindset

**Specific Ask**: "Would UF be able to help facilitate introductions to 2-3 protocol teams that are working on custom pool economics or hook implementations?"

---

#### Q5: Validation Methodology & Feedback
**Question**: "What's the best way to validate if this is actually solving a real problem? Should I be doing user interviews, surveys, building a prototype first, or approaching it differently?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Gets expert advice on validation approach
- Ensures you're not building in a vacuum
- Demonstrates user-research mindset
- Opens discussion on UF support for validation

**Your Context**: "I have extensive design docs, but I want to validate before building. What would convince you this is worth pursuing?"

---

#### Q6: Async Validation for Busy Users
**Question**: "Most potential users are busy teams. What lightweight, async methods would work best for gathering feedback without asking for too much of their time? Short surveys, video demos, interactive docs?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Shows respect for user time
- Gets practical tactics for solo validation
- Demonstrates thoughtful research approach
- Identifies low-friction feedback mechanisms

---

### Part 4: Building Credibility as Solo Developer (15-20 min)

#### Q7: Credibility Without Reputation
**Question**: "I'm a solo developer with no industry reputation or job at a known company. In your experience, what helps unknown builders gain credibility in the Uniswap ecosystem? Are there specific milestones, contributions, or community activities that build trust?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Addresses the elephant in the room
- Gets practical credibility-building strategy
- Shows self-awareness
- Opens discussion on UF support mechanisms

**Potential Paths to Explore**:
- Community contributions (non-code)
- Educational content creation
- Testnet deployment with real usage
- Partnerships with known teams
- Speaking at events or workshops

---

#### Q8: Community Building & Visibility
**Question**: "From a marketing perspective, how should a solo developer gain visibility for an infrastructure project like this? Should I be focusing on Twitter, Discord, writing content, or other channels?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Gets marketing/community strategy advice
- Leverages her business/marketing expertise
- Builds foundation for future growth
- Shows long-term thinking

---

#### Q9: Storytelling & Positioning
**Question**: "How should I position this to the community? As a developer tool? As an accessibility solution? As v4 infrastructure? What messaging would resonate most with potential users and the broader ecosystem?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Gets positioning and messaging advice
- Taps into her marketing expertise
- Ensures clear value communication
- Helps with future marketing materials

---

### Part 5: Resources, Support & Partnership (15-20 min)

#### Q10: Grant Programs & Funding
**Question**: "Are there UF grant programs that support this type of ecosystem infrastructure work? What's the typical process, timeline, and what do successful applications look like?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Identifies funding opportunities
- Understands application requirements
- Gets insider perspective on what UF funds
- Sets realistic expectations

**Context to Share**: "As a solo dev with no savings, grant funding could enable full-time focus on this."

---

#### Q11: UF Support & Resources
**Question**: "Beyond funding, what other types of support does UF provide to ecosystem builders? Marketing support, technical connections, event opportunities, documentation access?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Discovers non-financial support options
- Identifies ecosystem resources
- Opens discussion on partnership possibilities
- Maximizes value from UF relationship

---

#### Q12: Ecosystem Partnerships & Collaboration
**Question**: "Are there other projects in the Uniswap ecosystem working on similar problems - accessibility, abstraction layers, or developer tools? Could there be collaboration opportunities rather than building in isolation?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Identifies potential partners
- Avoids duplicating work
- Shows collaborative mindset
- Gets warm introductions to related projects

---

#### Q13: Technical Connection (Despite Her Background)
**Question**: "While I know you focus on business/marketing, would UF be able to connect me with someone technical who could provide occasional architecture feedback? Even quarterly reviews would help ensure I'm on the right track."

**Why This Helps**:
- Acknowledges her background respectfully
- Still seeks technical validation pathway
- Shows awareness of what she can/can't help with
- Opens door to broader UF team

---

### Part 6: Goals, Success Metrics & Next Steps (10-15 min)

#### Q14: Success Criteria from UF Perspective
**Question**: "From Uniswap Foundation's perspective, what would make this project 'successful'? Real usage? Number of pools deployed? Educational impact? Something else?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Aligns on success definition
- Understands UF's evaluation criteria
- Sets realistic goals
- Creates accountability framework

---

#### Q15: Timeline & Milestones
**Question**: "I'm planning a 1-month MVP sprint to build a working proof of concept. What milestones or deliverables during that time would be most valuable to share with you? And what cadence of updates makes sense?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Sets clear expectations
- Establishes update rhythm
- Shows execution focus
- Creates accountability touchpoints

**Proposed Cadence**: "2-week check-in and end-of-month demo?"

---

#### Q16: Next Steps & Follow-up
**Question**: "What are the concrete next steps from here? Should I:
1. Build the MVP first, then reconnect?
2. Do user validation first, then build?
3. Apply for a grant, then execute?
4. Something else?

What would you recommend as the most effective path forward?"

**Why This Helps**:
- Gets clear action items
- Ensures you're following UF's preferred process
- Establishes follow-up structure
- Shows willingness to adapt approach

## Key Talking Points to Weave In

### Your Unique Value (Despite Solo Status)
1. **User-Focused Research**: "I've spent months researching the problem before writing code"
2. **Business + Technical**: "I bridge the gap - I understand both the technical side and the business need"
3. **Proven Problem**: "Economic theory and real market data validate this need"
4. **Execution Ready**: "I have a concrete plan to ship something in 30 days"

### Addressing Solo Developer Concerns
1. **Lean & Focused**: "Being solo means I can move fast and stay focused on solving one problem well"
2. **Community-First**: "I'm building this as public infrastructure, not a proprietary product"
3. **Open & Transparent**: "All research, code, and progress is public - nothing to hide"
4. **Seeking Partnership**: "I'm not trying to do everything alone - actively seeking feedback and collaboration"

### Value Proposition to Uniswap Ecosystem
**The Business Case**:
1. **Adoption Driver**: More teams can deploy pools → more v4 usage
2. **Market Expansion**: Opens v4 to non-technical teams (bigger TAM)
3. **Innovation Catalyst**: Easier experimentation → more innovative pool designs
4. **Ecosystem Moat**: Better DX than competitors → keeps builders on Uniswap

**Use Simple Analogies**:
- "Stripe made payments accessible → explosion of online businesses"
- "Shopify democratized e-commerce → millions of new stores"
- "This could do the same for custom DeFi pools on Uniswap"

## Success Metrics for This Interview

1. ✅ **Problem Validation**: Confirm there's real demand from her community insights
2. ✅ **User Access**: Get pathway to 3-5 potential users for validation
3. ✅ **Credibility Strategy**: Clear plan for building reputation despite solo status
4. ✅ **Support Clarity**: Know what UF can/can't help with
5. ✅ **Next Steps**: Have concrete action items and follow-up scheduled
6. ✅ **Messaging**: Clear positioning based on her marketing expertise

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

❌ **Don't**: Use technical jargon (hooks, deltas, reactive networks, etc.)
✅ **Do**: Use business analogies and simple language

❌ **Don't**: Dive into architecture details she can't evaluate
✅ **Do**: Focus on user value and market opportunity

❌ **Don't**: Ask for technical validation she can't provide
✅ **Do**: Ask for business/marketing/community insights

❌ **Don't**: Assume she knows Uniswap v4 technical details
✅ **Do**: Explain concepts in business terms

❌ **Don't**: Position as pure "developer tool"
✅ **Do**: Position as "market expansion opportunity"

---

## Conversation Adaptations

**If she says**: *"I'm not technical enough to evaluate this"*
**You say**: "That's actually perfect - my whole point is this should make sense to non-technical people. Let me show you the before/after..."

**If she says**: *"This sounds complex"*
**You say**: "The underlying tech is complex, but the user experience is simple. Think of it like how Stripe handles payments - complex behind the scenes, simple to use."

**If she says**: *"How is this different from existing tools?"*
**You say**: "Right now, to customize a pool you need a blockchain developer. This is like going from 'hire a developer' to 'use a no-code tool' - same outcome, way more accessible."

**If she says**: *"Prove there's demand"*
**You say**: "That's exactly why I'm here - I need your help accessing potential users to validate this. Would UF be open to facilitating 3-5 user interviews?"
