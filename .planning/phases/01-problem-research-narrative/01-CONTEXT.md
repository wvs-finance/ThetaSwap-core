## Implementation Decisions

### Problem framing
- Opening hook: "There's a latent risk that discourages passive liquidity providers — and no current hedging instrument tracks it on-chain"
- Frame as **adverse competition**, not healthy competition — reference Capponi, Jia & Zhu (2024) on JIT substituting (not complementing) passive LPs, which decreases market quality
- Concrete example: walk through one passive LP's experience in ETH/USDC 30bps — deposits, JIT enters, fee share drops from 1/N to x_k << 1/N, eventually exits
- Two key risk properties to emphasize:
  1. **Path-dependent tail risk** — proven by econometrics (rare but severe: 1 trigger day in 41)
  2. **Completely orthogonal to IL/LVR** — can be hedged independently of price/market risk
- Use data from both the reference papers and our own research, showing our data confirms what papers predict

### Literature references (problem section)
- All five papers cited explicitly:
  - **Capponi, Jia & Zhu (2024)** — JIT crowds out passive LPs, substitution vs complementarity
  - **Capponi & Zhu (2024)** — optimal exit thresholds, why concentrated fees cause LP exits
  - **Ma & Crapis (2024)** — symmetric equilibrium / competitive null (what "fair" looks like)
  - **Aquilina et al. (2024, BIS)** — 7% of LPs capture 80% of fees (empirical severity)
  - **Bichuch & Feinstein (2024)** — FLAIR metric, fee-as-derivative pricing (backs our metric design)

### Research depth
- **Full equation + coefficient table**: quadratic hazard model (main.pdf eq. 9), lag 1-3 results table (Section 2.2), turning point formula (eq. 10)
- **Summarized econometric interpretation from main.pdf Section 5.7**: inverted-U two regimes — shelter regime (below δ*) vs Capponi regime (above δ*)
- **Marginal effect at turning point** (Section 5.7.2) and **insurance pricing translation** (Section 5.7.3)
- Research summary serves three pillars — each statistic supports one:
  1. **Demand exists** — negative β₁ flipping to positive β₂ proves contingent demand when Δ > δ*
  2. **A price exists** — marginal effect → implied premium ($110/position at Δ=0.15)
  3. **Backtest validates optimal scenarios** — p² payoff shows 18.8% of positions better off hedged, positive mean hedge value

### Narrative structure
- Flow: **Problem → Evidence → Solution → Demo → Roadmap** (classic pitch arc)
- One idea per Beamer frame (sparse, fast pace)
- Flat frames (no \section navigation bar)

  - Generated by re-running notebooks headless (`make notebooks`)

  - Context diagram (FCI Hook, Vault, CFMM, Protocol Adapters, Reactive Network)
