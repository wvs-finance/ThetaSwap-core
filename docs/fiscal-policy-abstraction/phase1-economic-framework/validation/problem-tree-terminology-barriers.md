# Problem Tree: Terminology Barriers to Traditional Finance Onboarding

## Root Hypotheses

### **Primary Hypothesis**
*"Not unified terminology for designing economic incentives on-chain vs traditional finance prevents onboarding of traditional finance professionals who are still taught the traditional way in colleges and universities."*

### **Secondary Hypothesis**
*"Problems solved under the same framework of optimal taxation have multiple equivalent solutions in DeFi, but the lack of unified terminology prevents traditional finance professionals from recognizing and implementing these solutions."*

### **Tertiary Hypothesis**
*"People from traditional finance onboarding to crypto looking to implement something that requires a taxation system would benefit from having the same terminology instead of learning new equivalent solutions."*

### **Quaternary Hypothesis**
*"People employed in crypto have to learn new terminology that can be adapted to classical terminology if abstraction layers are put on, enabling better communication and collaboration with traditional finance practitioners."*

---

## 1. Problem Tree Structure

### 1.1 Root Problems

#### **Primary Problem**: 
Traditional finance professionals cannot effectively design or implement economic incentives in DeFi due to terminology barriers.

#### **Secondary Problem**: 
Multiple equivalent DeFi solutions exist for traditional finance problems, but traditional finance professionals cannot recognize or implement them due to terminology barriers.

#### **Tertiary Problem**: 
Traditional finance practitioners looking to implement taxation systems in crypto would benefit from familiar terminology rather than learning new equivalent solutions.

#### **Quaternary Problem**: 
People employed in crypto have to learn new terminology that can be adapted to classical terminology if abstraction layers are put on, enabling better communication and collaboration with traditional finance practitioners.

#### **Issue 1: Educational Gap**

#### **Issue 4: Professional Development Gap**
